About Me

I am a Master’s student in the Mechanical Engineering Department at Eidgenössische Technische Hochschule (ETH) Zürich, passionate about Robotics, Systems and Control. I am driven by challenging research questions in this field and the opportunity to make a positive impact on people’s lives. Currently, I am developing a digital twin of an operating room to enable seamless evaluation of surgeon training. In this project, I explore human pose estimation and state estimation. I am also an assistant on the organising team of the Robotics Summer School, conducted by RobotX at ETH Zurich.

I completed my Bachelor’s degree at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Madras, where I graduated at the top of my class and won the best thesis award. During my time there, I developed a speed-control submodule, an autonomous system on ROS, and a prototype of a compliant hand exoskeleton.

Education

  • ETH Zürich — Master of Science ETH in Mechanical Engineering, 2024–Present
  • IIT Madras — Bachelor of Technology (Honors) in Mechanical Engineering with a Minor in Systems Engineering, 2020–2024

Research Interests

I am deeply interested in the intersection of robotics and human well-being, with a particular focus on:

  • Robotics for Rehabilitation and Assistive Technology
  • Medical Robotics (Perception, System Control, and Modeling)

As part of my current and future research, I am looking forward to collaborating with peers and professors at ETH Zurich in projects related to these fields.
